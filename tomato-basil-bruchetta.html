Tomato Basil Bruschetta Bruschetta is an Italian antipasto dish which means starter dish. It always includes grilled bread with garlic and olive oil but there are many different variations. Common toppings include tomato, cheese, beans, cured meat or vegetables. Bruschetta originated in Italy during the 15th century. Although it can be traced to ancient Rome. Olive growers owuld bring their olives to an olive press and taste a sample of their oil using a slice of bread. Ingredients 2 mini-loaves Italian bread, cut into 1/2 inch diagonal slices 8 Roma tomatoes, chopped and drained 2 garlic cloves, minced 1/2 red onion, chopped 6 fresh basil leaves 2 Tablespoons oil Salt to taste Pepper to taste Directions Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Place bread on a baking sheet in a single layer. Bake about 5 to 7 minutes until it begins to brown slightly. Combine tomatoes, garlic, onion, basil and oil in a bowl. Season with salt and black pepper, to taste (if you wish). Place toasted bread on a serving platter. Serve the tomato mixture in a bowl with a serving spoon and let everyone help themselves.
